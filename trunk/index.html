Title: Germplasm Vocabulary (Darwin Core extension for genebanks)
Abstract: The Germplasm Vocabulary (Darwin Core extension for genebanks) was developed to provide a bridge between the established standards in use by the genebank community for plant genetic resources for food and agriculture (PGRFA) and the standards maintained by the Biodiversity Information Standards/Taxonomic Databases Working Group (TDWG). The Germplasm Vocabulary provides an extension to the Darwin Core standard of the TDWG for occurrences. Darwin Core can itself be seen as an extension to the Dublin Core Metata Data Initiative (DCMI) terms.

The primary objective for creating the Germplasm Vocabulary was to describe the additional terminology required for exchange of genebank datasets using the GBIF Integrated Publishing Toolkit (IPT). The terms included in the DwC germplasm are from the Multi-Crop Passport Descriptors (MCPD) and the proposed descriptors for germplasm characterization and evaluation data (C&E) (see: [EPGRIS3_WORKSHOP] and [EPGRIS3_C&E_TERMS]) from the EPGRIS3 group of the European Cooperative Programme for Plant Genetic Resources (ECPGR).

The (now obsolete) prototype version 0.1 of the DwC germplasm was developed by the Nordic Genetic Resources Center (NordGen and Bioversity International (Bioversity) in August 2009.
